Sound familiar

You know some of the money is going nowhere.
You just can't point at where.

Wasted spend is rarely one obvious mistake. It's a handful of quiet decisions nobody reviews. Here's the kind of thing an audit surfaces.

Attribution laundering
Your "best" campaign might be taking credit, not creating sales.
Retargeting shows a stellar ROAS by claiming sales from people who were going to buy anyway. On the dashboard it looks like your winner. In reality it's borrowing credit from the campaigns doing the real work.
Shift that budget to spend that creates new customers. Same budget, more growth.
The learning phase trap
Ten ad sets starving where one would thrive.
Meta needs roughly 50 conversions a week per ad set to optimise properly. Split your budget across too many ad sets and none of them ever get there, so the algorithm guesses instead of learns. The account looks busy and performs beneath its potential.
Consolidate and the same spend buys cheaper customers. No new budget required.
Missing exclusions
Paying to acquire customers you already have.
When existing customers aren't excluded from acquisition campaigns, part of every day's budget goes to people who already bought. It inflates your ROAS and quietly shrinks the share of spend reaching genuinely new buyers.
Exclude them and every acquisition dollar works on actual new customers.
Profitable restraint
Beating targets while spending 39% of the budget.
A recent audit found an account beating its CPA target by $2 while spending just 39% of its stated monthly budget. Nothing was broken, which is exactly why nobody was looking. The growth was sitting there unclaimed.
Scale what's already working. The cheapest growth is the budget you're not spending.

The free agency audit
A free audit isn't an audit. It's a pitch deck.

Agencies give audits away because the audit isn't the product, your retainer is. So it finds enough wrong to justify a proposal, skips whatever doesn't lead there, and lands on the same recommendation every time: hire us. It's rarely dishonest. It's just built to sell.

You're paying for the answer, not for the pitch. Sometimes the answer is that your account is in decent shape and your agency is doing fine. A free audit can't tell you that.
The AI audit
AI does the analysis well. It's the filter on top you're paying for.

Point a good model at your account and it will find real issues. That part works, and we use those tools too. What it hands back is a long list with no sense of proportion: every flag looks equally urgent, and none of it knows your brand, your margins, your market, or what Meta changed last month.

Michael has seen hundreds of accounts. That's the filter. Which flags matter for a brand like yours, which are noise, and what's actually worth doing first.

Questions

Common questions.

Is this a sales pitch in disguise?
No. The audit is a standalone product with a fixed scope: report, action plan, walkthrough call. You can action everything in it yourself, hand it to your agency, or ask us about working together. All three are fine outcomes.
Who actually does the audit?
Michael, personally. The framework and the data pull are consistent from account to account, which is the point: nothing gets skipped. What isn't automated is the part that matters, which is reading your account in context and deciding what's actually worth your time. He also presents the findings to you on the call.
What do I actually walk away with?
A PDF report you keep. Scores for all 7 areas, the evidence behind each one, your Top 3 fixes, and the full action plan. It's yours: share it with your team, hand it to your agency, or work through it yourself. Nothing expires and nothing is held back behind a follow up call.
Why would I pay when agencies audit for free?
Because a free audit is a sales asset. It exists to win a retainer, so it's shaped around that outcome. This one is the product itself, which means it can conclude that your account is healthy, that your agency is doing good work, or that the fix has nothing to do with us.
Couldn't I just run my account data through an AI tool?
You can, and the analysis will be decent. We use those tools in the process too. What you won't get is proportion: a model treats every flag as equally urgent and has no read on your brand, your market, your margins, or what changed on the platform last month. That filter is what you're paying for.
What access do you need?
Read only. You grant view performance access through Meta Business Settings, which lets us see the data without any ability to change anything. Instructions are in the intake form.
Does this work for lead generation, or just ecommerce?
Both. The 7 areas are the same, but what good looks like changes. Lead gen accounts get assessed on lead quality feedback loops and cost per customer, not just cost per form fill.
What if my account is too small to audit?
The audit is designed for accounts spending $3k or more a month. If your account genuinely doesn't have enough data to audit properly, we'll tell you before starting and refund you in full.
Do you keep access to my account afterwards?
No. Once the walkthrough call is done, remove our partner access in Business Settings. We'll remind you on the call.
What do you need from me during the 5 days?
Usually nothing beyond the intake and access. If something material is missing, like a landing page URL or a tracking screenshot, we'll ask once, in one message.
